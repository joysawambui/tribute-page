Proffesor Wangari Muta Maathai

Internationally renowned Kenyan environmental political activist and Nobel laureate

Below are the life and times of Proffesor Wangari Maathai:

  • 1940: Born in Ihithe, Nyeri, Kenya
  • 1948: Joined primary school
  • 1960: Maathai became one of some 300 Kenyans selected to study in the United States through the Africa Airlift program by J.F.Kennedy Foundation.
  • 1964: Obtained a bachelor of science degree from Mount St. Scholastica College
  • 1966: Maathai received her M.Sc in biological sciences,and was appointed to a position as research assistant to a professor of zoology at University College of Nairobi(UoN). Met Mathai, who would become her husband. Established a general store where her sister’s worked
  • 1969: Got married to Mathai, got pregnant with first child, husband run for electoral office and lost.
  • 1971: She became the first Eastern African woman to receive a PhD, her doctorate in veterinary anatomy,from the University College of Nairobi, completed her dissertation and had her second child.
  • 1973: Became the director of the Kenya Red Cross Society
  • 1974: Mathai run for parliamentary seat and won, Wangari assisted him in fulfilling the promise of reducing unemployment by founding Envirocare Ltd., a business that involved the planting of trees. Resulted in the first tree nursery at Karura Forest. Envirocare Ltd, however, failed due to lack of funding among other issues.
  • 1975: Became the first female Senior lecturer in anatomy at UoN
  • 1976: became the first female Chair of the Department of Veterinary Anatomy at UoN and She attended the first UN conference on human settlements, known as Habitat I
  • 1977: became the First female Associate professor at UoN. All this time, she had been campaigning for women’s equal work benefits and formed a union to rally this cause. ON World Environmental Day, she led a procession planting trees in the Central Business District of Nairobi. This became the first “Green Belt Movement”.
  • 1979: Filed for divorce from her husband after a long seperation from 1977. Husband cited infidelity and inability to control Wangari as reasons. She served three days in jail for contempt of court when she insulted the judge during an interview with a local magazine. Left bankrupt after the costly divorce. Shortly after became chairperson of the National Council of Women of Kenya (NCWK)
  • 1982: Wanted to for parliamentary seat in Nyeri. Court dismissed her eligibility on a technicality. She had resigned from her job at the UoN to run as required by law. She was left jobless and homeless since her home was in the university.
  • 1986: The Green-belt movement with funding from UNEP, expanded throughout Africa and led to the foundation of the Pan-African Green Belt Network.
  • 1987: Maathai was forced to step down from Chairperson of NCWK to focus on the Green belt movement.
  • 1989: Maathai opposed the construction of the 60-story Kenya Times Media Trust Complex in Uhuru Park. She was referred to as “mad” and “ignorant” and was told to “sit down and be quiet like a proper African Woman”. Despite all this, the foreign investors withdrew their plans. The government audited The Green Belt Movement in hopes of shutting it down and even forced her to move it into her home by taking away her office.
  • 1991: She received the Goldman Environmental Prize in San Francisco and the Hunger Project's Africa Prize for Leadership in London.
  • 1992: Maathai learnt of a government plot to assassinate her and others also a state-funded coup. She was arrested but shortly after released. She led people in a “hunger strike” to campaign for release of political prisoners.
  • 1997: Maathai was running for elections. Rumours of her withdrawing and endorsing another candidate went public the morning of the election so she lost.
  • 1998: Stopped a government plan to give away forested land to political supporters. She actively opposed the move and got attacked and injured for it. The international community got wind of this and it sparked outrage.
  • 2001: Another ploy of the government to give public land to political supporters was foiled by Maathai. She was detained and later released. She returned to teaching at the Yale University's School of Forestry and Environmental Studies
  • 2002: She returned to Kenya, run for parliamentary seat for Tetu Constituency and won.
  • 2003: Maathai was appointed Assistant Minister in the Ministry for Environment and Natural Resources. She founded Mazingira Green Party of Kenya allowing candidates to run on a platform of conservation.
  • 2004: Wangari Maathai was awarded the 2004 Nobel Peace Prize for her "contribution to sustainable development, democracy and peace”.
  • 2011: Wangari Maathai died of complications arising from ovarian cancer while receiving treatment at a Nairobi hospital.

"Maathai stood up courageously against the former oppressive regime in Kenya. Her unique forms of action have contributed to drawing attention to political oppression—nationally and internationally. She has served as inspiration for many in the fight for democratic rights and has especially encouraged women to better their situation.”

— The Norwegian Nobel Committee, in a statement announcing her as the 2004 Nobel Peace Prize winner.
